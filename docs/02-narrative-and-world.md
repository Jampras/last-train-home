# 02 - Narrative and World

## Premise

The world once tolerated wandering cat communities that lived around rail lines, old stations, and service depots. Something changed. Humans now organize capture squads and use trained dogs to hunt, scatter, or cage cats. A moving train became the only way some cats could survive together.

The player begins as a child under the care of their father, the current leader of the sanctuary train. During a protected stop, a catastrophic night attack destroys the illusion of safety. The father dies protecting the community. Three years later, the child has grown into the train's new leader.

## Story Goals

- create a strong opening wound
- establish the father as both mentor and mystery
- frame the train as family, home, and burden
- motivate exploration with clues, not exposition dumps
- reveal that the opening disaster was not random

## Prologue Structure

### Scene 1 - Safe Arrival

Player enters a protected station.

Teach:

- move left and right
- interact
- gather simple items
- light a lantern
- use the train as a safe anchor

The father guides the player naturally. Tutorial text should be light and in-character.

### Scene 2 - Routine Before Dusk

The player helps with everyday tasks.

- carry oil to a lamp
- greet another cat
- feed a small stray
- place a simple barricade
- return before night

This section establishes warmth and norms.

### Scene 3 - The Disaster

The attack breaks the rules of the tutorial space.

- a unique alarm sound triggers
- lights fail in sequence
- dogs appear too close to the perimeter
- humans breach the station
- the father orders the player into a safe compartment
- the player watches the father stay behind
- the screen cuts away before explicit violence

### Scene 4 - Time Skip

Text card:

- `3 years later`

The train is patched, quieter, and harder. The player is now the active leader.

## World State

The train follows unstable routes between stations, depots, yards, and forgotten platforms. Cats use these locations to scavenge, hide, recruit, and restore old infrastructure long enough to survive a few nights.

Humans control checkpoints, kennels, signal towers, and cargo lines. Some humans are hostile because they profit from capture. Others fear the cats for reasons tied to the broader mystery.

## Suspense Backbone

The player should slowly learn:

- the father knew more than he revealed
- the sanctuary train carries something important
- the opening attack was targeted
- certain stations contain records of coordinated hunts
- some merchants are informed intermediaries, not neutral outsiders

## Narrative Delivery Methods

- brief in-run dialogue
- station memories triggered at key landmarks
- wagon conversations between runs
- found notes or signal logs
- visual storytelling in stations
- unlock text for cats, wagons, and relics

Avoid long unskippable dialogue blocks.

## Factions

### The Sanctuary Cats

The moving community on the player's train.

Core values:

- mutual care
- practical survival
- rebuilding
- secrecy

### Capture Crews

Human teams that hunt or contain cats.

Common tools:

- lanterns
- nets
- cages
- whistles
- bait

### Kennel Units

Dog handlers and trained dogs used to track, corner, and break defensive lines.

### Night Traders

Traveling outsiders who visit stations at night. Some are helpful. Some are suspicious. Some may connect to the larger mystery.

## Main Character

Working role:

- the child of the former leader
- now a young adult cat carrying inherited responsibility

Key traits:

- empathetic
- observant
- not yet fully confident
- shaped by a remembered failure

## The Father

Narrative function:

- tutorial guide
- emotional anchor
- lost protector
- source of unresolved questions

The father should not become a one-note martyr. Through later discoveries, the player learns both admirable and questionable choices he made to keep the train moving.

## Story Arc for Version 1

### Act 1 - Taking Command

- finish first true station run
- stabilize train basics
- recruit first named cats
- discover clues that the prologue attack was planned

### Act 2 - Traces of the Hunt

- unlock harder stations and faction variants
- meet merchants with incomplete information
- recover logs, route maps, and hidden cargo references

### Act 3 - What the Train Carries

- learn why the train is being pursued
- identify who ordered the prologue strike
- survive an extended station crisis
- secure the next route and preserve the community

Version 1 should answer enough to feel satisfying, but still leave room for future chapters.
