# 01 - Game Vision

## One-Line Pitch

A side-view run-based settlement survival game where a young cat leader guides a moving sanctuary train across hostile territory, rescues survivors, and rebuilds the community after a childhood tragedy.

## High Concept

The player leads a train full of cats through a world where humans and trained dogs hunt or capture them. Each station becomes a temporary run:

- arrive with limited people and supplies
- explore left and right
- recruit cats and gather resources
- build a refuge around the train
- survive eventful nights
- leave with rewards, unlocks, and new train growth

The player does not lose permanent progress on failure. Instead, failure ends the current station run while still feeding the train-wide meta progression.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Visible Progression

The player must see progress after nearly every run.

- new cat unlocked
- new train wagon built
- new upgrade path opened
- new station type discovered
- new event class added to the run pool

### 2. Gentle Tension

The game should create suspense, not exhaustion.

- easy and medium modes are forgiving
- early runs emphasize discovery
- danger escalates slowly
- staying longer in a run is tempting, not punishing by default

### 3. Warmth Against Threat

The game world must feel beautiful and worth protecting.

- the train is cozy, soft, and lived-in
- stations are lonely, abandoned, and atmospheric
- nights feel uncertain and risky
- returns to the train feel emotionally restorative

### 4. Character-Led Variety

Each cat leader changes the way the player approaches a run.

- each leader has a signature train wagon or station upgrade
- each leader has a mastery challenge
- mastering a leader unlocks an advanced form of their specialty

### 5. Clear Side-View Readability

All core actions must be readable in a horizontal 2D layout.

- movement left and right
- resource pickup
- interaction
- expansion state
- defense line state
- event prompts
- enemy approach direction

## Player Fantasy

The player should feel:

- responsible
- clever
- protective
- resourceful
- more capable after every journey

The emotional arc is:

- child under protection
- survivor of a formative loss
- uncertain leader
- trusted builder of a new home

## Tone

Primary tone:

- gentle melancholy
- quiet suspense
- warmth in hardship
- resilience through community

Avoid:

- gore
- cynical comedy
- oppressive difficulty on default modes
- grimdark presentation

## Target Audience

- players who enjoy soft survival loops
- players who like visible unlock progression
- players who like cozy-but-tense games
- fans of side-view management games
- players who want meaningful web-playable sessions

## Session Targets

- prologue: 10 to 15 minutes
- early station run: 15 to 20 minutes
- normal station run: 25 to 35 minutes
- long station run: 40 to 50 minutes
- between-run train management: 5 to 10 minutes

## Difficulty Modes

### Aconchegante

- slower clock pressure
- more positive events
- softer enemy waves
- easier recovery from mistakes
- lower material costs

### Jornada

- intended default
- steady but fair pressure
- meaningful event variety
- moderate resource friction
- mastery systems fully relevant

Hard mode can exist later, but it is not required for first release.

## Core Success Metrics

The game meets vision targets if players commonly report:

- "I always unlocked something"
- "The train keeps getting better"
- "I want to stay one more night"
- "The next cat changes how I play"
- "The opening tragedy stayed with me"
